ABOUT ME

Building Intelligent Security Systems

Bridging theoretical research with real-world applications in vehicular network security

I'm a PhD researcher at the College of Computing Department at Mohammed VI Polytechnic University, working at the cutting edge of vehicular network security and artificial intelligence.

My research focuses on developing post-detection mitigation frameworks for Vehicular Ad-hoc Networks (VANETs) using Graph Neural Networks and Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning. I formulate complex security challenges as Markov Decision Processes and employ advanced techniques like Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) to reduce false positives and false negatives in anomaly detection systems.

With a strong foundation in machine learning, deep learning, and security, I'm passionate about creating practical solutions that make autonomous vehicle networks safer and more reliable. My work combines rigorous academic research with hands-on implementation across various domains including NLP, computer vision, and IoT security.

Beyond research, I serve as a Teaching Assistant and TPC Member, contributing to the academic community while continuously pushing the boundaries of what's possible in intelligent security systems.

Graph Reinforcement Learning for Secure Vehicular Networks

Mohammed VI Polytechnic University (UM6P) • October 2023 - Present

In Progress

Developing a post-detection mitigation framework for Vehicular Ad-hoc Networks (VANETs) to handle uncertainties in anomaly detection systems. Formulated the problem as a Markov Decision Process over dynamic vehicular graphs and employed Graph Neural Networks combined with Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) to prune malicious V2V links and reduce false positives and false negatives.
